\subsection{Composer 2.5: targeted textual feedback through on-policy distillation
\citep{cursor2026composer25}}
\paragraph{Source type.} First-party research article.
\paragraph{Research question.} Ask how a localized mistake inside a rollout spanning many
turns and tool calls can receive a useful training signal when the terminal reward is too diffuse.
\paragraph{Method.} For a problematic model turn, insert a short corrective hint into the
teacher context while leaving the student's context unchanged, then apply an on-policy KL
loss that moves the student token distribution toward the hint-conditioned teacher distribution.
\paragraph{Main evidence.} Record reported behavioral uses, benchmark changes, synthetic-task
scaling, reward-hacking observations, and any available ablation of targeted feedback.
\paragraph{Connection to the position.} Provide a direct systems example of combining
trajectory-level RL with dense, localized, privileged-context feedback.
\paragraph{Critical assessment.} Examine who generates and verifies hints, whether the
teacher correction is valid, tokenization and support mismatch, interference with the RL
objective, selective reporting, and the lack of full training details.
\paragraph{Takeaway.} Complete after reading.
