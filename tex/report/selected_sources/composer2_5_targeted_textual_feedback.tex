\subsection{Composer 2.5: targeted textual feedback through on-policy self-distillation
\citep{cursor2026composer25,shenfeld2026selfdistillation}}
\paragraph{Source type.} First-party research article.
\paragraph{Research question.} Ask how a localized mistake inside a rollout spanning many
turns and tool calls can receive a useful training signal when the terminal reward is too diffuse.
\paragraph{Method.} Run the same policy in two roles at a problematic student turn: an
ordinary-context student and a self-teacher that additionally sees a validated,
non-answer-leaking hint. Apply an on-policy KL loss that moves the ordinary distribution
toward the hint-conditioned distribution.
\paragraph{Main evidence.} Record reported behavioral uses, benchmark changes, synthetic-task
scaling, reward-hacking observations, and any available ablation of targeted feedback.
\paragraph{Connection to the position.} Provide a direct systems example of combining
trajectory-level RL with dense, localized, privileged-context feedback.
\paragraph{Critical assessment.} Examine who generates and verifies hints, whether they
leak unavailable task information, whether the induced correction is valid, interference
with the RL objective, selective reporting, and the lack of full training details.
\paragraph{Takeaway.} Complete after reading.
